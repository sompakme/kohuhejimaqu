Do impressions make you gag?

Making an impression requires inserting a tray filled with an unset impression material into a patient's mouth and removing it once set. For the majority of patients, this process is well tolerated; however, for some it can elicit a strong gag reflex.

Are dental impressions painful?

Can you swallow dental impressions?

Don't swallow

No doubt about it, having an impression tray in your mouth will cause you to salivate. You may be tempted to swallow the excess saliva, but that may trigger your gag reflex. Instead, don't be afraid to drool. It's not elegant, but you'll be less likely to gag.

Is gagging at the dentist normal?

Gagging is a normal response that nearly half of the patients who visit the dentist say they've experienced. For people with a healthy gag reflex, they can control gagging, but for others, they may have little or no control over it.
