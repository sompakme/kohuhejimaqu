Prahlad Modi (born in 1953; Age: 69 years old) is a well-known shopkeeper, media face, politician brother, and internet personality from Vadnagar, Gujarat, India. He is the younger brother of the Indian Prime Minister and former Gujarat Chief Minister named Narendra Modi.

Who is Prahlad Modi, Narendra Modi’s Brother?

Modi never took advantage of being the brother of the Indian Prime Minister and led a very simple lifestyle. He was working happily as the owner of a ration shop in Ahmedabad but got retired in 2021.

He often grabbed the limelight for protesting against various policies and strategies, even though those were made by his brother Narendra Modi. Keep Reading this article to find out more about Narendra Modi’s younger brother Prahlad Modi.

Prahlad Modi Accident and Latest News

According to the official sources, Prahlad Modi met in a car accident when he was traveling to Bandipur with his son, daughter-in-law, and grandson. The accident occurred on 27 December 2022 (Tuesday) near Mysuru, Karnataka. Reportedly, Prahlad’s Mercedes Benz car lost control and hit the divider.

The car was not overspeeding. All the family members were taken to the JSS Hospital for treatment and they are safe. Modi and other family members sustained minor injuries but his grandson had a leg fracture.

Career, Business, and Protests

Prahlad Modi completed his education and started working in the region. Afterward, he owned a ration shop in Ahmedabad but retired in 2021 because of his old age. In addition to this, he is also serving the position of vice president of the All India Fair Price Shop Dealer’s Federation. It was founded in 2001.

Prahlad is responsible for distributing ration at reasonable prices to poor people and families. In addition to this, he also protested against the government for its policies. In August 2022, he staged a dharna at Delhi’s Jantar. The federation wants compensation for loss on rice, wheat, and sugar to be supplied through fair-price shops.

Also, they raised slogans and banners and wanted their 9 demands to be accepted. Prahlad never stepped back from protests even after the Indian government is led by his brother Narendra Modi.
