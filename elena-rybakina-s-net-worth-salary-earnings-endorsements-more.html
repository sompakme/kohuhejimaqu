Elena Andreyevna Rybakina is a professional tennis player from Kazakhstan. She holds the prestigious title of reigning Wimbledon champion, making her the first player from Kazakhstan to achieve such a remarkable feat at a major tournament.

Moreover, Elena has made history by reaching an impressive career-high singles ranking of world No. 3, and is currently ranked No. 4, according to the WTA, as a Kazakhstani player. Throughout her career, Rybakina has displayed exceptional talent and consistency, reaching an impressive thirteen finals on the WTA Tour.

In addition to her 2022 Wimbledon triumph, she has secured four more titles, including two highly esteemed WTA 1000 titles at the 2023 Indian Wells Masters and the 2023 Italian Open.

Rybakina advanced to the quarterfinals of Wimbledon in 2023 as the defending champion before falling to Ons Jabeur in three sets. She advanced to the semifinals at the 2023 Canadian Open for the first time in her career after losing in the quarterfinals of Wimbledon. Prior to defeating Daria Kasatkina in the match that lasted 3 hours and 27 minutes, Rybakina defeated Jennifer Brady and Sloane Stephens.

Due to the scheduling challenges she had during the event, Rybakina claimed in the post-match press conference that she felt "destroyed" physically when she lost to Ludimilla Samsonova in the quarterfinals in three sets.
