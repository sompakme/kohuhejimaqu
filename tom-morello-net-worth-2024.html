Tom Morello’s Early Life

Tom Morello was born in Harlem, New York, and raised in Libertyville, Illinois. His interest in music began at a young age, and he received his first guitar at the age of 13. Morello’s unique style of playing was influenced by heavy metal bands like Black Sabbath and Led Zeppelin.

Music Career

Morello’s music career took off when he co-founded the band Rage Against the Machine in 1991. The band’s blend of heavy metal, punk, and hip hop, combined with their politically charged lyrics, made them a sensation. Morello’s innovative guitar playing was a significant part of the band’s success.

Rage Against the Machine

Rage Against the Machine released their self-titled debut album in 1992, which went on to achieve triple platinum status. The band released three more successful albums before disbanding in 2000.

Audioslave

After the disbandment of Rage Against the Machine, Morello formed Audioslave with former Soundgarden vocalist Chris Cornell. Audioslave released three albums, all of which were commercially successful.
