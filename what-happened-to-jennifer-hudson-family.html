The Hudson Family Tragedy

On October 24, 2008, the world was shocked to learn that Jennifer Hudson’s mother, Darnell Donerson, her brother, Jason Hudson, and her seven-year-old nephew, Julian King, were found dead in Chicago. The devastating news came at a time when Hudson’s career was on a meteoric rise following her Oscar win for her role in “Dreamgirls.”

The Day of the Murders

The bodies of Hudson’s mother and brother were discovered in Donerson’s home on Chicago’s South Side. Both had been fatally shot. Julian King was missing from the scene, prompting an urgent search that ended in heartbreak. Three days later, the young boy’s body was found in a parked vehicle several miles away, also a victim of gun violence.

The Investigation

As the investigation unfolded, it became clear that this was a case of domestic violence turned deadly. The prime suspect was quickly identified as William Balfour, the estranged husband of Hudson’s sister, Julia. Balfour had a troubled past, including a history of domestic disputes and a criminal record.

The Trial and Conviction

William Balfour’s trial began in April 2012, nearly four years after the murders. The prosecution built a case around Balfour’s threats against the family, his jealous rage, and the physical evidence linking him to the crimes. In May 2012, Balfour was convicted on all counts and subsequently sentenced to life in prison without the possibility of parole.
